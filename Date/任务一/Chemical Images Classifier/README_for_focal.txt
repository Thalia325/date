Figure 1

Figure 2
Script: both panels were generated from the data by the script make_plot.R.
Data: data were simulated by the script linked_load.R and saved in the format "coefficient_=_X1_diffnum_=_X2_trials_=_X3_generations_=_X4_outcrossingrates_=_X5_X6_X7_popsize_=_X8_chromnum_=_X9_proportionA_=_X10_distance_=_X11_cohortmultiplier_=_X12.csv" where X1, X2, etc. correspond to the arguments input into linked_load.R to generate the data.
X1, "coefficient," refers to the selection coefficient at each locus, where fitness was 1, 1, 1 - s for the superior homozygote, the heterozygote, and the inferior homozygote.
X2, "diffnum," refers to the number of pairs of loci alternatively fixed for superior and inferior alleles in the two parental populations.
X3, "trials," refers to the number of simulations run for a particular parameter combination to generate the fixation proportion, and it was held constant at 2000.
X4, "generations," refers to the maximum number of generations the simulation would run before stopping and producing an error, and it was held constant at 1000.
X5, X6, and X7, "outcrossingrates," refer to the outcrossing rates of the more selfing homozygote, the heterozygote, and the more outcrossing homozygote at the modifier locus.
X8, "popsize" refers to the number of adults in each generation before selection has removed a fraction of the seeds (i.e., an adult carrying capacity was always reached each generation), and it was held constant at 100.
X9, "chromnum" refers to the number of chromosomes across which the viability loci were distributed.
X10, "proportionA" refers the initial frequency of the genotype on which the outcrossing mutation arose.
X11, "distance" refers to the recombination fraction between adjacent loci in the genome.
X12, "cohortmultiplier" refers to the ratio of seeds to adults (e.g., "10" means 10 seeds for every 1 adult, but not every adult necessarily produced any seeds), and it was held constant at 10.
Each data file contains a single number, the fixation proportion out of all X3 trials for the parameter combination listed in the filename.
After linked_load.R generated the data, tabulate_data.R put them all into a two .csv files called fixation_point_five.csv and fixation_point_one.csv, named for the recombination fraction.
Two parameter combinations failed to run to completion the first time.
The outcomes for these two parameter combinations were re-simulated with calculate_fixation_holes.R, and the tabulated data were edited with plug_holes.R.
The data for X11 ("distance") = 0.5 were also used to generate Figure 3.

X1, "coefficient," refers to the selection coefficient at each locus, where fitness was 1, 1, 1 - s for the superior homozygote, the heterozygote, and the inferior homozygote.
X2, "diffnum," refers to the number of pairs of loci alternatively fixed for superior and inferior alleles in the two parental populations.

X9, "chromnum" refers to the number of chromosomes across which the viability loci were distributed.

Figure 4
Script: all panels were generated from the data by the script depression_traj_plotter.R.
Data: data were simulated by the script depression_trajectory.R.
Data were saved in three formats.
The genotype trajectories of modifier genotype frequencies were saved in the format, "genotypeTrajectory_coefficient_=_X1_diffnum_=_X2_trials_=_X3_generations_=_X4_outcrossingrates_=_X5_X6_X7_popsize_=_X8_chromnum_=_X9_proportionA_=_X10_distance_=_X11_cohortmultiplier_=_X12_depressioninterval_=_X13_depressionsamplesize_=_X14_trialNum_=Y.csv" where X1, X2, etc. correspond to the arguments input into depression_trajectory.R to generate the data, and where Y is a number assigned to each different replicate of the same parameter combination.
The trajectories of mean population fitness were saved in the format, "aveFitnessTrajectory_coefficient_=_X1_diffnum_=_X2_trials_=_X3_generations_=_X4_outcrossingrates_=_X5_X6_X7_popsize_=_X8_chromnum_=_X9_proportionA_=_X10_distance_=_X11_cohortmultiplier_=_X12_depressioninterval_=_X13_depressionsamplesize_=_X14_trialNum_=Y.csv"
The trajectories of inbreeding depression were saved in the format, "depressionTrajectory_coefficient_=_X1_diffnum_=_X2_trials_=_X3_generations_=_X4_outcrossingrates_=_X5_X6_X7_popsize_=_X8_chromnum_=_X9_proportionA_=_X10_distance_=_X11_cohortmultiplier_=_X12_depressioninterval_=_X13_depressionsamplesize_=_X14_trialNum_=Y.csv"
X1, "coefficient," refers to the selection coefficient at each locus, where fitness was 1, 1, 1 - s for the superior homozygote, the heterozygote, and the inferior homozygote.
X2, "diffnum," refers to the number of pairs of loci alternatively fixed for superior and inferior alleles in the two parental populations.
X3, "trials," refers to the number of simulations run for a particular parameter combination to generate the fixation proportion, and it was held constant at 2000.
X4, "generations," refers to the maximum number of generations the simulation would run before stopping and producing an error, and it was held constant at 1000.
X5, X6, and X7, "outcrossingrates," refer to the outcrossing rates of the more selfing homozygote, the heterozygote, and the more outcrossing homozygote at the modifier locus.
X8, "popsize" refers to the number of adults in each generation before selection has removed a fraction of the seeds (i.e., an adult carrying capacity was always reached each generation), and it was held constant at 100.
X9, "chromnum" refers to the number of chromosomes across which the viability loci were distributed.
X10, "proportionA" refers the initial frequency of the genotype on which the outcrossing mutation arose.
X11, "distance" refers to the recombination fraction between adjacent loci in the genome.
X12, "cohortmultiplier" refers to the ratio of seeds to adults (e.g., "10" means 10 seeds for every 1 adult, but not every adult necessarily produced any seeds), and it was held constant at 10.
X14, "depressioninterval" refers to the waiting time between successive estimates of inbreeding depression, and it was held constant at 1 (inbreeding depression estimated every generation).
X15, "depressionsamplesize" refers to the number of selfed and outcrossed offspring (which numbers must be equal) to generate for the sample from which inbreeding depression is estimated.
Each genotype trajectory file contains four columns: one for the generation and three for the frequencies of the three modifier genotypes.
Each row represents the frequencies of the modifier genotypes in that generation.
Each mean fitness trajectory has four columns: one for the generation and three for the mean fitness of individuals with each of the three modifier genotypes.
For both the genotype and mean fitness trajectory, the first column represents homozygotes for the selfing allele, the second column represents heterozygotes, and the third column represents homozygotes for the outcrosssing allele.
There is one row for each generation that the outcrossing and selfing allele were both segregating.
Each inbreeding depression trajectory file consists of two columns: one for the generation and one for the population inbreeding depression
The genotype and fitness trajectories and final outcomes for 5 and 25 pairs of viability loci generated using this script are not shown in the manuscript.
The inbreeding depression trajectories for 5 and 25 pairs of viability loci are shown elsewhere, in Fig. S5.
The inbreeding depression data in Fig. 4 a subset of those in Fig. S5 (the distribution in the final generation for 50 pairs of viability loci).
